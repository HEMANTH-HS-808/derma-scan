## Getting Started

### Initial Login

1. **Open the Application** - Launch DermaScan Pro in your web browser
2. **Select Your Role** - Choose between:
   - **User** - For medical professionals and patients seeking diagnostics
   - **Admin** - For laboratory administrators and system management
3. **Click Login** - The system will authenticate your role and grant access

### Navigation

- **Logo** - Click the DermaScan Pro logo anytime to return to the home page
- **Top Navigation Bar** - Access main features based on your user role
- **Logout Button** - Found in the top-right corner, use it to securely exit

## How to Use

### Step 1: Upload an Image

1. Navigate to the **Diagnostics Dashboard**
2. Click the **Upload Image** button
3. Select a clear, well-lit image of the skin lesion
4. **Image Requirements:**
   - Clear and focused
   - Good lighting
   - Lesion centered in frame
   - High resolution recommended

### Step 2: Initiate Analysis

1. After upload, click **Analyze** or **Scan**
2. Wait for the AI to process the image (typically 5-15 seconds)
3. The system will display a loading indicator during processing
4. Results will appear automatically upon completion

### Step 3: Review Results

1. Check the **Primary Diagnosis** at the top
2. Review the **Severity Level** indicator
3. Examine **Risk Score** (higher = more critical)
4. Study the **Clinical Indicators** breakdown
5. Read the detailed disease description

### Step 4: Access Treatment Information

1. Scroll down to view **Treatment Recommendations**
2. Review suggested treatment options
3. Note important clinical considerations
4. Consult with a medical professional for treatment decisions

### Step 5: Generate Report

1. Click **Generate Report** or **Export**
2. Choose format (PDF/Print)
3. Save or print for medical records
4. Share with healthcare providers if needed

## Supported Skin Conditions

### üî¥ Melanoma (Critical Risk)
**Severity:** Critical | **Risk Score:** 9/10

- **Description:** Life-threatening form of skin cancer originating from melanocytes
- **Characteristics:** Asymmetry, irregular borders, multiple colors
- **Clinical Indicators:** High across all ABCD parameters
- **Treatment:** Wide local excision, lymph node biopsy, immunotherapy (PD-1 inhibitors)

---

### üü° Basal Cell Carcinoma (Moderate Risk)
**Severity:** Moderate | **Risk Score:** 6/10

- **Description:** Most common skin cancer type arising from basal cells
- **Characteristics:** Pearly or waxy bump appearance
- **Clinical Indicators:** Moderate ABCD scores
- **Treatment:** Mohs micrographic surgery, excisional surgery, topical chemotherapy

---

### üü¢ Benign Keratosis (Low Risk)
**Severity:** Low | **Risk Score:** 2/10

- **Description:** Non-cancerous growths with characteristic waxy appearance
- **Characteristics:** Waxy, brown, black, or tan, appears "stuck on"
- **Clinical Indicators:** Low ABCD scores
- **Treatment:** Cryotherapy, curettage, or electrocautery (often cosmetic only)

---

### üîµ Melanocytic Nevi (Benign)
**Severity:** Benign | **Risk Score:** 1/10

- **Description:** Common moles, benign melanocyte accumulations
- **Characteristics:** Uniform color, smooth borders
- **Clinical Indicators:** Minimal concerning features
- **Treatment:** No treatment required unless changes occur; regular monitoring recommended

## Understanding Results

### Risk Score Interpretation

| Score | Risk Level | Action Required |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1-2   | Benign | Monitor for changes; routine follow-up |
| 3-4   | Low Risk | Periodic monitoring; annual check-ups |
| 5-6   | Moderate | Medical evaluation recommended; consider treatment |
| 7-8   | High | Urgent medical consultation; likely treatment needed |
| 9-10  | Critical | Immediate medical intervention required; specialist referral |

### ABCD Clinical Indicators

**A - Asymmetry (0.0 - 1.0)**
- 0.0 = Perfectly symmetrical
- 1.0 = Highly asymmetrical (concerning)

**B - Border (0.0 - 1.0)**
- 0.0 = Well-defined, smooth border
- 1.0 = Irregular, jagged borders (concerning)

**C - Color (0.0 - 1.0)**
- 0.0 = Uniform color
- 1.0 = Multiple distinct colors (concerning)

**D - Diameter (0.0 - 1.0)**
- 0.0 = Small diameter (< 6mm)
- 1.0 = Large diameter (> 6mm, concerning)

## Tips for Best Results

### Image Quality

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Use high-resolution images (3MP or higher)
- Ensure clear, natural lighting
- Center the lesion in the frame
- Maintain focus on the affected area
- Include scale reference if possible

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Use blurry or out-of-focus images
- Photograph in shadows or poor lighting
- Include unnecessary background
- Use filters or image editing
- Apply makeup or ointments to the area

### Before Analysis

- Clean the skin area gently
- Allow any perspiration to dry
- Remove jewelry or accessories
- Take images at the same location/time for consistency
- Document location on body for future reference

### Documentation

- Note the date of analysis
- Record any recent changes
- Track lesion growth or color changes
- Keep a history for comparison
- Share results with dermatologists

### Medical Consultation

‚ö†Ô∏è **Important:** This tool is for **diagnostic assistance only**. Always:
- Consult with a qualified dermatologist for final diagnosis
- Don't rely solely on AI analysis for critical decisions
- Seek immediate medical attention for rapidly changing lesions
- Report any concerning symptoms to healthcare providers
- Follow up on high-risk findings promptly

## Troubleshooting

### Image Won't Upload
- Check file format (JPG, PNG supported)
- Verify file size is under 10MB
- Ensure internet connection is stable
- Try a different browser if issues persist

### Slow Analysis
- Check your internet connection
- Wait for server processing to complete
- Clear browser cache if needed
- Try again with a different image

### Unclear Results
- Verify image quality and lighting
- Reupload with better focus
- Ensure proper center positioning
- Contact support if problems continue
